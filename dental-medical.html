FAQ

Questions from Dental & Medical Practices

Conduit is designed with healthcare privacy in mind. The AI does not store protected health information (PHI) beyond what's necessary for call handling. Call data is encrypted in transit and at rest, and our platform follows industry best practices for healthcare data handling. We recommend consulting with your compliance officer to ensure our solution meets your specific regulatory requirements.

Conduit does not provide medical or dental advice. Instead, it follows your practice's after-hours protocols. For routine matters (scheduling, billing questions), it handles them directly. For clinical concerns, it captures the patient's symptoms and details, provides your practice's standard after-hours guidance (e.g., “go to the ER for severe bleeding”), and escalates to your on-call provider when criteria are met.

Yes. You configure the insurance plans you accept, common billing information, and payment policies. When patients call asking “Do you accept Delta Dental?” or “What's the cost of a cleaning?”, Conduit provides accurate answers based on your configuration. Complex billing inquiries are flagged for your front desk to handle during business hours.

Conduit integrates with popular practice management systems including Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Open Dental, Athenahealth, and others. Appointments booked by Conduit appear directly in your PMS, eliminating double-entry. We also support Google Calendar, Outlook, and webhook-based integrations for custom workflows.

True emergencies are handled according to your protocols. You define your escalation criteria — for example, severe pain, uncontrolled bleeding, facial swelling, or post-surgical complications. When Conduit identifies an emergency based on these criteria, it immediately escalates to your on-call provider via text, call, or both, while providing the patient with your emergency instructions.
